The Perfect Ribeye Steak

Say hello to the most juicy, tender, and perfect ribeye that you have ever seen. Perfectly seasoned and perfectly cooked. A true masterpiece. Skip the expensive reservations and grab that cast iron.

Ingredients (4 Servings)

  • 1 bone-in ribeye steak, 2 inches thick, at room temperature (24-ounces)
  • Kosher salt and freshly ground black pepper (to taste)
  • 1 tbsp of canola oil
  • 3 tbsp of unsalted butter
  • 3 cloves of garlic, smashed
  • 3 sprigs fresh thyme
  • 2 sprigs fresh rosemary

Directions

  1. Use a paper towel and pat both sides of the steak dry. Season generously with 1 1/4 teaspoons of salt and 1/2 teaspoon of pepper
  2. Heat a medium cast iron pan over medium high heat until very hot and add canola oil
  3. Place the steak in the middle of the pan and cook, flipping the steak every 2-3 minutes, until a dark crust has formed on both sides. (12-14 minutes)
  4. Reduce the heat to mediumn low and push the steak to one side of the pan. Add butter, garlic, thyme, and rosemary to the opposite side of the pan.
  5. Cook while tilting the pan towards the butter and cook until the butter is foaming. (30 seconds - 1 minute)
  6. Carefully spoon the butter over the steak for 1-2 minutes, turning it over once, until it reaches an internal temperature of 120 degrees fahrenheit for a perfect medium rare
  7. Make sure to let the steak rest for 15 minutes before slicing.
  8. Serve and enjoy!
